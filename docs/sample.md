# Large Language Models (LLMs)

Large Language Models (LLMs) are advanced artificial intelligence systems trained on vast amounts of text data to understand and generate human-like language. They work by learning patterns in words, sentences, and context rather than memorizing exact answers. Built on the Transformer architecture, LLMs process text using tokens (small chunks of words) and convert them into numerical representations called embeddings. Through mechanisms like attention, the model determines which parts of a sentence are important and uses this understanding to generate meaningful responses, summaries, translations, or code.

During training, LLMs learn by predicting the next word in a sequence repeatedly across billions of examples, gradually improving their understanding of language structure and meaning. At inference time, when you ask a question, the model generates responses one token at a time based on probability distributions learned during training. This allows LLMs to perform a wide range of tasks without task-specific programming, making them highly flexible tools for applications such as chatbots, document analysis, recommendation systems, and intelligent assistants.
